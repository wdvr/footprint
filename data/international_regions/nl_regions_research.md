# Netherlands Administrative Divisions - ISO 3166-2:NL

## Overview

The Netherlands (European territory) is divided into 12 provinces. The Kingdom of the Netherlands also includes three autonomous constituent countries in the Caribbean (Aruba, Curacao, and Sint Maarten) and three special municipalities (Bonaire, Saba, and Sint Eustatius), but for travel tracking purposes, the 12 European provinces are the primary divisions.

**Total Provinces:** 12

## ISO 3166-2:NL Code Structure

Each code consists of two parts separated by a hyphen:
- First part: `NL` (ISO 3166-1 alpha-2 code for Netherlands)
- Second part: Two letters derived from the province name

## Historical Notes

- Flevoland is the youngest province, formed on January 1, 1986, from reclaimed land (polders) in the former Zuiderzee
- The codes have been in use since the ISO 3166-2 standard's initial publication in 1998
- The 2010 constitutional reform dissolved the Netherlands Antilles and restructured the Kingdom to include Aruba, Curacao, and Sint Maarten as constituent countries
