# Argentina Provinces - ISO 3166-2:AR Research

## Overview

Argentina is divided into **24 administrative divisions**:
- 23 provinces (provincias)
- 1 autonomous city (Ciudad Autonoma de Buenos Aires)

## Special Notes

### Buenos Aires City vs Buenos Aires Province

Argentina has two distinct administrative divisions with "Buenos Aires" in their names:

1. **AR-C - Ciudad Autonoma de Buenos Aires (CABA)**: This is the federal capital and autonomous city of Argentina. It was formerly known as the Federal District and gained autonomous city status following constitutional reforms in 1994. It has a special status similar to the provinces but is legally distinct.

2. **AR-B - Buenos Aires Province**: This is the largest and most populous province in Argentina, surrounding (but not including) Buenos Aires City. The capital of Buenos Aires Province is La Plata, not Buenos Aires City.

### ISO Code Letter Assignment

The ISO 3166-2 codes use single letters derived from the old vehicle registration plate system and postal codes. Note that:
- Letters **I** and **O** are not used (to avoid confusion with numbers 1 and 0)
- The codes are not alphabetically sequential by province name

### Tierra del Fuego Full Name

The full official name of Tierra del Fuego province is "Tierra del Fuego, Antartida e Islas del Atlantico Sur" (Tierra del Fuego, Antarctica and South Atlantic Islands), reflecting Argentina's territorial claims in Antarctica.
