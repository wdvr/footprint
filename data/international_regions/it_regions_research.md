# Italy Administrative Divisions - ISO 3166-2:IT

## Overview

Italy is divided into 20 regions (regioni), of which 5 have special autonomous status and 15 are ordinary regions. The regions are the first-level administrative divisions. Each region is further divided into provinces or metropolitan cities.

**Total Regions:** 20 (15 ordinary + 5 autonomous)

## ISO 3166-2:IT Code Structure

Each code consists of two parts separated by a hyphen:
- First part: `IT` (ISO 3166-1 alpha-2 code for Italy)
- Second part:
  - Two digits for regions (e.g., IT-21 for Piedmont)
  - Two letters for provinces and metropolitan cities (e.g., IT-MI for Milan)

The first digit of regional codes indicates the geographic macro-region:
- **2** = Northwest
- **3** = Northeast
- **4** = Emilia-Romagna / Liguria
- **5** = Central
- **6** = South (Lazio, Abruzzo, Molise)
- **7** = South (Campania, Puglia, Basilicata, Calabria)
- **8** = Islands

## 5 Autonomous Regions with Special Statute

These regions have broader legislative, administrative, and fiscal powers under the Constitution:

| ISO Code | Region | Reason for Special Status |
|----------|--------|--------------------------|
| IT-23 | Valle d'Aosta | French-speaking minority |
| IT-32 | Trentino-Alto Adige | German-speaking minority (South Tyrol) |
| IT-36 | Friuli-Venezia Giulia | Slovenian-speaking minority, border region |
| IT-82 | Sicily | Island, unique cultural identity |
| IT-88 | Sardinia | Island, unique cultural identity |

### Note on Trentino-Alto Adige

Trentino-Alto Adige (IT-32) includes two autonomous provinces that operate with regional-equivalent autonomy:
- **IT-TN** - Trento (Trentino)
- **IT-BZ** - Bolzano (South Tyrol / Sudtirol)

These are the only provinces with regional-level powers.

## Historical Notes

- The regional system was established by the Constitution of the Italian Republic (1948)
- Valle d'Aosta split from Piedmont in 1948
- Molise split from Abruzzi in 1963 (effective 1965)
- The 2001 constitutional reform (Title V) enhanced regional powers
- The 2014 Delrio Law created metropolitan cities and reduced provincial powers
