# Russian Federal Subjects - ISO 3166-2:RU Research

## Overview

Russia is divided into **83 internationally recognized federal subjects** (as of ISO 3166-2):
- 21 republics (respublika)
- 9 krais (administrative territories)
- 46 oblasts (administrative regions)
- 2 federal cities
- 1 autonomous oblast
- 4 autonomous okrugs (districts)

**Note**: Russia claims 89 federal subjects including disputed territories annexed from Ukraine (Crimea, Sevastopol, Donetsk, Luhansk, Kherson, Zaporizhzhia), but these are not included in ISO 3166-2:RU as they are internationally recognized as Ukrainian territory.

## Special Notes

### Disputed Territories (NOT in ISO 3166-2:RU)

The following territories are claimed by Russia but are **NOT included in ISO 3166-2:RU** as they are internationally recognized as part of Ukraine:

1. **Republic of Crimea** - Annexed by Russia in 2014; internationally recognized as part of Ukraine (UA-43)
2. **Sevastopol** - Annexed by Russia in 2014; internationally recognized as part of Ukraine (UA-40)
3. **Donetsk Oblast** - Claimed annexed in 2022; internationally recognized as part of Ukraine
4. **Luhansk Oblast** - Claimed annexed in 2022; internationally recognized as part of Ukraine
5. **Zaporizhzhia Oblast** - Claimed annexed in 2022; internationally recognized as part of Ukraine
6. **Kherson Oblast** - Claimed annexed in 2022; internationally recognized as part of Ukraine

**Recommendation for Footprint app**: Follow the ISO standard and do not include these disputed territories as Russian regions. Users who visit Crimea and other disputed areas should mark them under Ukraine.

### Nested Subdivisions

Three autonomous okrugs are technically within oblasts but still count as separate federal subjects:
- Khanty-Mansi (RU-KHM) and Yamalo-Nenets (RU-YAN) are within Tyumen Oblast (RU-TYU)
- Nenets (RU-NEN) is within Arkhangelsk Oblast (RU-ARK)

### Similar Names - Be Careful

- **Altai Republic (RU-AL)** vs **Altai Krai (RU-ALT)** - Two distinct regions
- **Moscow City (RU-MOW)** vs **Moscow Oblast (RU-MOS)** - City and surrounding region

### Historical Mergers

Between 2003-2008, several federal subjects were merged, reducing the count from 89 to 83. The ISO codes reflect the current post-merger structure.
